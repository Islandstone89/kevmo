Intro

Kevin Moore was a vital part of Dream Theater's early history. A childhood friend of John Petrucci, he joined the band in 1986, where he stayed till 1994. During his time the band released 3 studio albums, with Moore penning 8 out of 27 songs.

Discography

6 March 1989

When Dream and Day Unite

Recorded in 3 weeks during the summer of 1988, at Kajem/Victory Studios in Gladwyne, Pennsylvania. The album did not sell particularly well, with only a handful of shows to follow, most of them local. Later that year, they fired Charlie Dominici, leading to an extensive 14-month search for their new singer.

7 July 1992

Images and Words

Recorded between October and December 1991 at BearTracks Studios in Suffern, New York. This was the album that kickstarted the career for Dream Theater, when the opening song "Pull Me Under", penned by Moore, unexpectedly became a radio hit. A video was quickly shot, and the band spent most of 1993 on the road, including two trips to Europe and Japan.

4 October 1994

Awake

Recorded between May and July 1994 at One on One Studios, North Hollywood, California. At the end of the recording process, on July 6, Moore told the band members that he wanted to leave the band. In a press release on 22 August, Moore said:

"It came to a point where my views were so different from the rest of the band that we were having trouble relating to each other's ideas. At the same time, I was finding a great deal of fulfillment writing and recording my own material. Eventually, this became more important to me than anything.
